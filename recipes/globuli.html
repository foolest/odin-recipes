Globuli (sweet fried curd cheese)

Petronius, a Roman courtier in the first century CE, wrote that the students of his day â€œlearn nothing but "melitos verborum globulos et omnia dicta factaque quasi papavere et sesamo sparsa" which in English is "honey-sweetened round globs of words and all things said and done as if sprinkled with poppy seed and sesame seed". Apparently, this insult made perfect sense to Romans because students were familiar with the delicious fried food called globuli.

Ingredients

  • 500g (about 1lb) curd cheese
  • 1 cup semolina
  • honey
  • olive oil

Steps

  1. Drain the curd cheese. Use a sieve or colander, let it hang in cheesecloth, or squash excess moisture out.
  2. Mix with the semolina into a loose dough and let it sit for a few hours.
  3. With wet hands, form the mixture into dumplings
  4. Quickly fry dumplings in olive oil for a few minutes.
  5. Drain and roll in honey.
